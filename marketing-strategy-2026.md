# Gist — New Marketing / ASO Strategy (July 2026)

> Written 2026-07-03. Supersedes the "bridge positioning / sports small talk" thesis in `aso-plan.md`.
> App: **Gist** (ID `6770138156`). Status today: **0 tracked keyword rankings, 2 US ratings** = effectively invisible.

---

## 1. Diagnosis — why there are no downloads

This is **not** a keyword-tuning problem. It is a **channel/positioning** problem.

**The core finding:** the app is optimized for a keyword cluster that has almost no search volume.
- Astro's AI keyword engine, run on this exact app, could surface only **two** candidates: `gist` (pop 33, a generic word) and `talking points` (pop 56, polluted by edu/political apps). It found nothing else worth suggesting. That is the semantic space collapsing.
- The team's own data already showed the whole target cluster is dead: `conversation starters` (pop 24), `small talk` (pop 9), `icebreaker` (19). Winning "#1 for sports small talk" gets ~0 installs because ~0 people search it.
- The two escape routes are both closed:
  - **Head terms** (`sports news`, `scores`) = a wall of billion-rating incumbents: ESPN (4.0M ratings), theScore (840k), CBS (833k), The Athletic/NYT, Yahoo, Apple Sports. Difficulty 73-83. Unwinnable organically.
  - **"Beginner" terms** (`sports for beginners`) = **wrong intent**. That SERP returns Couch-to-5K, tai chi, sports *video games*, and meetup apps. Nobody searching it wants "understand sports to talk about it." Gist doesn't rank top-20.

**Conclusion:** the *intent* is enormous but **latent** — it never gets typed into App Store search. You cannot ASO your way to demand that isn't expressed as a query. So search must stop being the primary acquisition channel. Demand has to be **manufactured** off-store, then captured on-store.

Also: the live **description still says "The Sideline" six times** even though the app is now "Gist." That's a live credibility/conversion leak — fix immediately.

---

## 2. The reframe — from "clueless non-fan" to "casual fan who wants to keep up"

The current identity ("small talk for people who don't follow sports," "clueless," "non-fan") is a strategic mistake on three axes:
1. **Low search** — proven above.
2. **Self-deprecating / embarrassing** — nobody wants to *self-identify* as clueless, and nobody searches it. It caps the market at people willing to admit a deficiency.
3. **Tiny TAM** — "people who don't like sports" is a smaller, lower-intent pool than "casual fans."

**Reframe to the aspirational, larger identity:** the **casual fan** who wants to keep up without the time.
- Women are now **47% of the NFL fanbase, +9% YoY** — the fastest-growing, most under-served segment, and they are *fans*, not non-fans.
- This is exactly the **Morning Brew / Finimize / theSkimm** move: take an intimidating topic (finance/business) and make it feel easy and fun for smart, busy people. Morning Brew hit 100k subs in 3 months and a $75M exit — via social + referral, **not** search. Finimize: "understand the news in under 3 minutes." That is Gist's promise, applied to sports.
- New message: **"Sports, made simple. Keep up in 5 minutes a day."** Not "for dummies." For *the rest of us*.

---

## 3. The wedge — ride the 2026 World Cup, right now

The single highest-leverage move available, and it's time-boxed to the next ~2 weeks.

- The **FIFA World Cup 2026 is happening now** (June 11 – July 19, 2026) on **home soil** (US/Canada/Mexico). We're in the knockout rounds.
- It is the definitive **casual-fan gateway event**: 4 in 10 Americans plan to watch; Fox averaged **5M/match** in the group stage (a network record); USA games drew **22M+**; interest is led by **Gen Z (41%) and Millennials (35%)** — the exact "everyone's watching, I don't really follow" audience.
- **The App Store has no casual-explainer for it.** The `world cup` SERP is: FIFA's official app (2.8★), soccer *games*, Kalshi (betting), calendars, Panini sticker albums. **Zero** apps answer "what's happening, who do I root for, what does everyone at the bar know that I don't." That is a wide-open lane at peak demand.
- It's inherently **global** — the "I should follow this but I'm lost" feeling is universal, and it plugs into the per-store localization the app already has (MX/BR/ES/DE/FR/IT are prime soccer markets).

**Action:** ship a **World Cup / soccer mode** as the acquisition Trojan horse — "catch up on the tournament in 5 minutes," "who to root for," "what just happened." Then convert those casual-fan installs into year-round NFL/NBA/etc. usage after the final.

> Trademark caution: FIFA aggressively protects "FIFA" and "World Cup." Keep them **out of the app title**. Use descriptive **"soccer," "football," "the 2026 tournament," "2026"** in subtitle/keywords/screenshots and content. Many apps put "world cup" in keywords; it carries some risk — safer to lead with "soccer."

**This requires a content dependency:** the pipeline is currently US-sports/NFL-centric. World Cup mode needs soccer coverage in the briefing generator. Without it, global installs churn. This is the one build item that gates the whole wedge.

## 4. Channel strategy — manufacture demand, then capture it

Because there is no organic search demand, the funnel inverts. Search becomes the *capture* layer, not the *source*.

**Primary — short-form video (the unfair advantage).**
The content pipeline already generates daily briefings + card art. Repurpose them into TikTok / Reels / YouTube Shorts:
- "3 things to say about tonight's game." / "Sports coming up at work tomorrow? Here's your cheat sheet." / "The World Cup, explained in 60 seconds."
- This is literally Morning Brew's growth engine (content → audience → app), and Gist's content is *already produced daily*. Auto-cut vertical videos from the TL;DR + card art.
- This is what actually creates installs in a no-search category — and the resulting **install velocity feeds organic chart rank + brand search**, which is the only way to earn organic in this space.

**Paid — Apple Search Ads on the walled terms.**
You can't *rank* for `sports news` / `world cup` / `espn` / `the athletic`, but you can **rent** the top slot via ASA. That is the correct use of high-volume walled keywords: buy them, tightly measured, small budget, kill what doesn't convert. Also bid your own brand + competitor brands.

**Earned — Apple editorial featuring.**
Pitch Apple's editorial team a timely, human-angle story: "apps for World Cup newcomers" / "keep up with the tournament." Apple favors timely, well-designed, well-reviewed apps — Gist qualifies. Time the pitch to the WC window.

**Community — Reddit / group chats.**
r/soccer + r/NewToSoccer during the WC; r/nfl newbie threads; "explain like I'm 5" positioning. Shareable cards ("send this to the friend who doesn't get football") build the Morning-Brew referral loop.

## 5. Concrete ASO package (conversion-first, since search can't drive volume)

Because the title/keyword fields can't manufacture demand, optimize them for **clarity of promise + brand + the few realistically-capturable terms**, and pour effort into **screenshots + icon**, which convert the paid/social traffic.

**App name (30 max)**
- Recommended: **`Gist: Sports Made Simple`** (24)
- During WC window, alt: **`Gist: Soccer & Sports Simple`** (28) — grabs soccer browse without FIFA marks.

**Subtitle (30 max)** — pick by goal:
- Message-first (higher conversion): **`Keep up in 5 minutes a day`** (26)
- Keyword-leaning: **`Soccer & sports explained fast`** (30)

**Keywords (100 max, no spaces after commas, no title/subtitle repeats)**
```
soccer,football,world cup,explained,beginners,casual,recap,highlights,nfl,nba,catch up,rules,daily,fan
```
Rationale: cover the live event (soccer/football/world cup), the "make it easy" cluster (explained/beginners/casual/rules), and a couple of sport anchors (nfl/nba). Drop the walled head terms — rent those via ASA instead. (Flag: `world cup` carries minor TM risk in the field; drop if you want zero exposure.)

**Screenshots (the real conversion lever — first 2 do 90% of the work)**
People read captions, not UI. Big, bold, benefit-led captions over the warm retro art:
1. **Promise:** "Never feel lost when everyone's talking sports." (during WC: "The World Cup, finally makes sense.")
2. **Proof:** a real card — "What happened + why it matters, in plain English."
3. **Payoff:** "One thing to say to sound like you watched."
4. **Breadth:** the room/persona picker (party, work, date, family).
5. **Cadence:** "A fresh 5-minute briefing every day."

**Icon**
Every scores app is dark navy/black (ESPN, theScore, CBS). Differentiate on the shelf with the app's warm **cream/green/gold** house style and one simple bold mark. Test 2-3 in ASA — icon is a top-3 conversion driver.

**Description** — rewrite lead around the reframe and **remove every "The Sideline"**. Lead: "Gist gives you sports, made simple — catch up in five minutes so you can keep up with the game everyone's talking about." Keep the "not a scores/betting/fantasy app" disclaimer; it's good positioning.

## 6. Global

- The **World Cup is the global unlock** — universal event, universal "I'm lost but everyone's watching" feeling. Prioritize English stores (US/GB/AU/CA) + big soccer markets already localized (MX, BR, ES, DE, FR, IT).
- Globally, **lead with the sport, not the social anxiety.** The small-talk/watercooler framing is a US-office-culture artifact; it doesn't travel. Elsewhere: "football, finally makes sense."
- Hard dependency: global ASO needs **soccer/football content** in the pipeline. Don't drive installs to markets where the content is NFL-only — they'll churn and tank ratings.
