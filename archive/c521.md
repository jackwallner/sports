# The Sideline — UX Improvement Suggestions (C521)

This document analyzes the current codebase (`Sideline/`, `Shared/`, design specs in `docs/claude-design/`) and proposes improvements **only from the perspective of how a user operates and experiences the app**. No implementation is implied here.

**Who uses this app:** A non-fan who opens the phone for ~20 seconds before walking into a room—a parent, partner, or coworker who needs something to say and one question to ask, without following sports.

**Core user job:** Pick the *room* → read TL;DR + bullets + question → optionally verify a source → put the phone away feeling prepared.

---

## 1. First open: “What is this and am I in the right place?”

### What happens today
Cold launch goes straight to `TodayBriefingView` with Cocktail Party selected and a loading skeleton. There is no `OnboardingView` (design spec `01_onboarding.md` is still unbuilt). The user must infer product value from the briefing chrome and copy.

### User experience gaps
- **No orientation in the first 15 seconds.** Design calls for value prop → persona pick → optional team in under 15 seconds. A first-time user may not understand that chips are “rooms,” not sports categories.
- **Default persona may not match their life.** Everyone lands on Cocktail Party. Someone opening before a kid’s game or a work lunch may need Office or Moms and won’t discover that without tapping locked chips.
- **Sample data when Supabase isn’t configured.** `SampleBriefingService` keeps the UI usable in dev, but a real user with a misconfigured build would see plausible fake sports drama with no indication it isn’t live—damaging trust on first use.

### Suggested improvements (user-operable)
| Change | How the user experiences it |
|--------|------------------------------|
| Ship 3-page onboarding | They see “sound like you follow sports” once, pick the room that matches *their* situation, optionally name a team, then land on a briefing that already matches that choice. |
| Remember last-used persona | Next open starts in the room they actually use (e.g. Office Watercooler), not always Cocktail Party. |
| First-run hint on persona rail | One subtle line under the chips: “Switch room for how you’ll use this today” so they don’t treat chips as filters. |
| Clear “demo mode” if offline/sample | Banner: “Preview briefing—connect for today’s update” so they never rehearse fake lines at a real dinner. |

## 2. The 20-second briefing read (core loop)

### What happens today
Scroll layout: persona rail → hero (context label + headline + TL;DR) → numbered bullets → “Your move” question card → freshness footer. Pull-to-refresh reloads from network. This largely matches the design intent.

### User experience gaps
- **Headline vs TL;DR hierarchy is easy to misread.** Headline is smaller/secondary; TL;DR is the hero—good—but users scanning top-to-bottom may still read the eyebrow (“LEAD WITH THIS”) and headline first and miss the line they should say aloud.
- **Tag “why” is hidden from sighted users.** `tagReason` exists on bullets and is spoken in VoiceOver, but sighted users only see “DRAMA” / “NICE GUY” without the one-line explanation that makes the tag useful (“tea,” not cruelty).
- **Suggested question isn’t actionable in the moment.** Design debated copy/share; today there is no long-press to copy the TL;DR or question. Users often want to glance at the lock screen or paste into Notes before walking in.
- **No satisfying “you’re done” affordance.** Design asked for the question card to feel like a full stop; scrolling past it into a small footer can feel like the briefing continues.
- **Bullet body tap does nothing.** Only the source link opens Safari. Users who want “why did they bench him?” get no expansion (`BriefingDetailView` was spec’d as optional P2).

### Suggested improvements (user-operable)
| Change | How the user experiences it |
|--------|------------------------------|
| Visual “say this first” on TL;DR | They immediately know the bold block is what to lead with; headline becomes optional context. |
| Show tag reason inline (one line) | “Drama — locker room frustrated, per reporters” reads as gossip context, not a random label. |
| Copy on TL;DR and question | Long-press or share sheet copies the exact line they’ll say; less re-typing in the hallway. |
| Sticky or enlarged question footer zone | After the question, minimal UI—feels like “ready,” not more scrolling homework. |
| Optional bullet detail sheet | Tap bullet body → short sheet with full tie-in + tag note + big “Read source” for users who need 5 more seconds of confidence. |

## 3. Switching rooms (persona rail)

### What happens today
Horizontal chips; free users see lock icons on four personas; tap locked → paywall sheet, selection unchanged. Tap free → reload briefing. Accessibility labels are thoughtful.

### User experience gaps
- **Locked tap skips the “preview” gate.** Design (`08_pro_gate.md`) wants a teaser of what Office/Date Night *sounds like* before paywall. Today they jump straight to pricing—higher friction, weaker “I want that voice” moment.
- **No persona descriptions on the home screen.** `Persona.shortPitch` exists in code but isn’t shown. Users choose from five similar labels without “warm prompts for your kid” vs “safe for coworkers.”
- **Switching persona reloads with a hard cut.** No cross-fade or scroll preservation; can feel like a new app rather than the same briefing in a different voice.
- **Selected chip may scroll off-screen.** Design asked for auto-scroll-to-selected on appear; long labels on small phones hide which room is active.
- **Free user with one “real” chip can feel empty.** Design warned this must not feel broken; showing four locked rooms helps, but a single unlocked chip without copy explaining “more rooms with Pro” underuses the conversion surface.

### Suggested improvements (user-operable)
| Change | How the user experiences it |
|--------|------------------------------|
| Pro preview sheet before paywall | Tap Office → see blurred/teaser TL;DR in office tone → “Unlock this room” → paywall. They understand *what they’re buying*, not just a lock. |
| Subtitle under selected chip or hero | “Safe, current takes for coworkers” reinforces they picked the right room before they read bullets. |
| Light transition on persona change | Same scroll position, content cross-fades—feels like retuning, not navigating away. |
| Auto-scroll rail to selected chip | They always see which room is active without hunting horizontally. |

## 4. Pull to refresh and “am I up to date?”

### What happens today
`.refreshable` calls `viewModel.refresh()`. Free users on Cocktail Party always re-fetch; `refreshLimit` only appears if a non-Pro user somehow has a locked persona selected. The inline “You’re caught up” card describes Pro 3×/day but **free daily refresh limits are not enforced in code**—pull always hits the network again.

### User experience gaps
- **Mismatch between copy and behavior.** User reads “today’s free briefing is already the latest” but can pull again and get new network activity—confusing if backend also only updates once per day.
- **Refresh limit card doesn’t dismiss.** Design asked for non-nagging repeat pulls; card persists in `refreshLimit` state until state changes.
- **Pro value of “3×/day” isn’t visible on the briefing.** User doesn’t know if they’re on morning vs evening window; `refreshWindow` exists on `Briefing` but isn’t surfaced.
- **Settings “Manual refresh” doesn’t refresh.** Tapping the row only opens paywall for free users; Pro users see “Available” but the button doesn’t trigger `viewModel.refresh()`—dead control.

### Suggested improvements (user-operable)
| Change | How the user experiences it |
|--------|------------------------------|
| Align refresh rules with product promise | Free: one meaningful refresh per calendar day on Cocktail Party; further pulls show the upsell once per session, then stay quiet. |
| Show next update window | Footer: “Next refresh this evening (Pro)” or “Updated this morning”—sets expectations before they yank-to-refresh in the parking lot. |
| Wire Settings refresh to real reload | Pro user in Settings taps refresh → same briefing screen updates; trust that the control does something. |
| Dismiss or soften repeat upsell | Second pull same day: no new banner animation; maybe only a subtle footer line. |

## 5. Pro, paywall, and “is it worth it?”

### What happens today
Locked persona → `PaywallView` (RevenueCat remote or fallback). Settings shows Pro status, team picker (Pro), restore purchases. Fallback paywall has debug “Continue” for local store.

### User experience gaps
- **Paywall is generic, not room-specific.** User tapped “Date Night” but sees “Every Room, Covered”—correct structurally, weak emotionally. They wanted *that* conversation, not five abstract chips.
- **No post-purchase celebration or orientation.** After subscribe, sheet dismisses; user may not notice all chips unlocked or that team picker now matters.
- **Local team personalization is a dead end for content.** User picks “Philadelphia Eagles” in Settings, but `TodayBriefingViewModel` always fetches `scope: .national` and never reads `favoriteTeam`. Selecting Local Team persona still shows national scope—user thinks Pro is broken.
- **Restore purchases is buried in About.** Anxious subscriber who reinstalled looks in Settings → About; easy to miss before they churn.

### Suggested improvements (user-operable)
| Change | How the user experiences it |
|--------|------------------------------|
| Contextual paywall headline | From Date Night chip: “Sound prepared at dinner” + same three benefits; feels like completing the action they started. |
| First Pro moment checklist | After purchase: “Pick your team” + “Try Office Watercooler”—guided next taps, not a silent unlock. |
| Connect team → Local Team briefings | Picking Eagles changes what Local Team and local-scoped bullets talk about; otherwise Settings feels like placebo. |
| Restore at top of Settings / paywall | Reinstall path: one obvious “Already subscribed?” without hunting. |

## 6. Settings and persistence

### What happens today
Settings: Pro row, Local team (gated), Manual refresh (gated label only), Privacy/Terms/Restore/Version.

### User experience gaps
- **No “starting room” preference.** Onboarding isn’t there; Settings also doesn’t let them change default persona—power users must switch every launch.
- **Team list is NFL-only, long scroll.** User with a city or college loyalty may not find themselves; friction before Pro value lands.
- **No explanation when Local team is locked** beyond lock icon—same preview pattern as persona rail would help here too.

### Suggested improvements (user-operable)
| Change | How the user experiences it |
|--------|------------------------------|
| “Default room” in Settings | Sets which chip is selected on launch—matches how they actually use the app. |
| Search on team picker | Types “Eagles” instead of scrolling 32 teams in a hurry. |
| Inline copy on locked Local team row | “Pro biases briefings toward your market” + tap → preview or paywall, consistent with chips. |

## 7. Offline, errors, and trust

### What happens today
Failed fetch → SwiftData cache if available → offline banner + footer. No cache → empty state with technical `error.localizedDescription` and “Try again.” Source links open in-app Safari.

### User experience gaps
- **Duplicate offline messaging.** Banner (“Offline - showing the last update”) and footer say the same thing—noise on a already-anxious user.
- **Error copy is engineer-facing.** “Briefing request failed with status 401…” doesn’t tell them what to do (check connection, try later, contact support).
- **Stale briefing isn’t distinguished from fresh.** Footer shows relative time but not “this might be from yesterday”—important if they’re about to quote old news at work.
- **Source link truncation.** Long headlines truncate with no way to read full title before tap—minor trust hit when verifying.

### Suggested improvements (user-operable)
| Change | How the user experiences it |
|--------|------------------------------|
| Single offline indicator | One calm line; more vertical space for content they can still use. |
| Human empty states | “Can’t reach today’s briefing” + Retry + “Showing yesterday’s if available”—actionable, not diagnostic. |
| Stale threshold styling | >24h: footer shifts to amber “Updated yesterday—double-check before quoting.” |
| Source accessibility | Long-press headline for full title or two-line wrap in accessibility sizes. |

## 8. Accessibility and one-handed use

### What happens today
Solid VoiceOver labels on chips, question card, tags. Dynamic Type largely respected via `fixedSize` and multi-line text. Reduce Motion not explicitly handled for persona transitions.

### User experience gaps
- **Tag pills may clip at AX5.** Design flagged pill + source orphan risk; worth validating with largest content size.
- **Persona rail requires horizontal dexterity.** Standing in a hallway, one-thumb users may struggle to reach Office chip on the right.
- **No Reduce Motion path for refresh upsell.** Design spec asked fade-only when limit hits.

### Suggested improvements (user-operable)
| Change | How the user experiences it |
|--------|------------------------------|
| AX audit on bullet cards at largest size | Tags and sources remain readable without horizontal scrolling. |
| Optional “compact rail” or reorder by recency | Most-used room appears leftmost; less horizontal travel. |
| Respect Reduce Motion | Persona/refresh state changes use opacity only—less vestibular annoyance. |

## 9. Gaps between design intent and shipped UX

| Design spec | User expectation | Current app |
|-------------|------------------|-------------|
| Onboarding (`01`) | Fast orientation + right room | Missing |
| Pro gate preview (`08`) | Taste Pro voice before price | Paywall only |
| Briefing detail (`07`) | Tap bullet for more context | Source link only |
| Free 1×/day refresh (`10`) | Calm “caught up” after one refresh | Limit UI exists; gating incomplete |
| `GoalSettings` / team drives Local scope | Local briefings feel personal | `favoriteTeam` stored, not used in fetch |
| Question label “Ask them this” | Clear conversational cue | “Your move” (fine, but inconsistent with spec) |

Closing these gaps in priority order matches how users actually fail: **trust (live vs demo) → right room (onboarding/default) → Pro preview (conversion) → local team actually working (retention).**

## 10. Prioritized roadmap (user impact)

### P0 — Blocks the core job
1. **Onboarding + default persona** — User reaches the right briefing for their life in <20s.
2. **Wire Local team to real briefings** — Pro subscriber sees Eagles (or chosen team) in Local Team content, not national repackaging.
3. **Human error/offline copy** — User still trusts the app when the network fails.
4. **Pro preview before paywall** — User understands what locked rooms sound like before subscribing.

### P1 — Makes the core job easier
5. **Show tag reasons in UI** — Bullets land emotionally, not as cryptic pills.
6. **Copy TL;DR / question** — User carries exact lines without memorizing.
7. **Enforce free refresh rules + quiet repeat upsell** — Pull-to-refresh matches “caught up” promise.
8. **Remember last persona** — Repeat opens match habit.

### P2 — Polish and retention
9. **Bullet detail sheet** — Optional depth without becoming a news reader.
10. **Refresh window / next update in footer** — Pro users feel freshness worth paying for.
11. **Contextual paywall copy** — Conversion from the room they tapped.
12. **Team picker search + non-NFL paths** — Broader “local” identity (city-first copy).

---

## 11. What to deliberately not add (user clarity)

Per product spec, avoid UX that pulls users out of the 20-second job:
- Accounts, social feeds, scoreboards, fantasy, push notification settings in v1.
- Article bodies in-app (keep Safari as the verification step).
- Aggressive scarcity (“only 2 hours left!”) — users are already slightly anxious; calm upsell converts better.

---

## Summary

The app’s skeleton matches the **“pick a room → read → ask one question”** loop well. The biggest UX risks for a real user are **(1)** no first-run guidance, **(2)** Pro/local personalization that doesn’t change what they read, **(3)** conversion moments that show a lock instead of a voice preview, and **(4)** trust-breaking technical errors or demo content without labeling.

Improvements should be judged by one test: *Can someone who doesn’t follow sports open this once, lock their phone within 20 seconds, and walk in with three things to say and one question—feeling calm, not hustled?* Everything above is tied to that operational moment.
