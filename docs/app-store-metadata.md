## Description

The Sideline gives you quick sports pop-culture talking points before you walk into the room.

It is built for people who do not follow sports but still have sports people in their life: coworkers, partners, friends, kids, parents, and everyone at the party who suddenly has an opinion about a quarterback.

Open the app, pick the room you are walking into, and read a short briefing:

- One plain-English TL;DR you can say out loud.
- 3 to 6 talking points framed for non-fans.
- Pop-culture angles, drama, nice-guy stories, and redemption arcs.
- A source link on every point so you can check the original reporting.
- One question to ask a fan when you want to keep the conversation going.

The Sideline is not a scores app, betting app, fantasy app, or dense sports news feed. No standings. No stat tables. No team-logo wallpaper. Just enough context to feel included in the conversation.

The free version includes the Cocktail Party context with a daily national briefing.

The Sideline Pro unlocks all contexts, fresher briefings up to 3 times a day, and Local Team personalization.

## Keywords (100 characters max — comma-separated, no spaces)

```
conversation,starters,icebreaker,recap,nfl,nba,mlb,nhl,casual,fan,scores,news,daily,brief,trivia
```

(96 characters, 15 tokens — en-US.) The keyword set is **bridge positioning**, chosen
from Astro US-store data (2026-06):

- Sports-news head terms (news/scores/recap) are owned by ESPN/Yahoo/CBS at
  **difficulty 73-83** and most "sports X" long-tail sits at **popularity 5** (no volume).
- The winnable volume is the **conversation cluster**: talking points (pop 56),
  conversation starters (24 / diff 19), icebreaker (19 / 19), small talk (9 / 17) —
  a vertical otherwise owned by couples/party-card apps, not sports.
- Sideline owns the unique **intersection**: name carries "Sports Small Talk", subtitle
  carries "Talking points", keyword field carries the rest of the conversation cluster +
  league tokens + a few sports terms. Apple de-duplicates across fields, so no word repeats.

**Per-locale, not copy-paste.** Same-language storefronts are **differentiated** so each
carries the leagues its users search: en-GB (premier/cricket/rugby), en-AU (afl/nrl/footy),
en-CA (nhl/raptors/curling), es-MX (ligamx/béisbol) vs es-ES (laliga), pt-BR (brasileirão)
vs pt-PT (benfica/liga), fr-CA (lnh/hockey) vs fr-FR (ligue1). Indic stores lead with
cricket/IPL. Source of truth: the per-locale `fastlane/metadata/*/{name,subtitle,keywords}.txt`
files, regenerated by `scripts/aso-bridge-rewrite.py`.

Validate locally: `python3 scripts/validate-asc-metadata.py`
